Essential Tips for Tokyo

Tokyo is Japan’s busy and exciting capital. It’s a place where old traditions and modern life live side by side. You can see calm temples and shrines just steps away from bright neon lights and tall skyscrapers. There are quiet tea houses near fast bullet trains, and streets full of both history and new trends. Whether you enjoy exploring the past, shopping, trying new food, or going out at night, Tokyo has something for you.

    Things to Know:

  • Currency: Japanese Yen (JPY)
  • Language: Japanese (English is limited but signs in tourist areas are often bilingual)
  • Plug Type: Two-flat pin plug (Type A, sometimes Type B)
  • Safety: One of the safest cities in the world
  • Tipping: Not customary; in fact, it can be seen as impolite

When to Visit Tokyo

  • Spring (March to April) is famous for cherry blossoms – but it’s also the busiest time for tourism.
  • Summer (June to August) brings lively festivals like Sumida River Fireworks and colorful street parades, though it can be hot.
  • Autumn (October to November) is cooler, with beautiful red and gold foliage in gardens and temples.
  • Winter (December to February) is crisp and clear, with fewer crowds and great views of Mount Fuji on sunny days.

    • Tip: If you want to avoid heavy crowds but still enjoy pleasant weather, late autumn and early spring are ideal.

Getting Around

The best way to explore Tokyo is by train and subway. Trains are fast, safe, and always on time, with clear English signs at most stations. Consider getting a Suica or Pasmo card for easy travel. Taxis are clean and reliable but can be expensive, especially during late-night hours. Walking is also a great way to see hidden side streets and local life, especially in neighborhoods like Shimokitazawa or Yanaka.

Must-See Attractions in Tokyo

  1. Shibuya Crossing – The world-famous pedestrian scramble, best seen from above at Shibuya Sky.
  2. Tokyo Skytree –The tallest tower in Japan, offering sweeping views of the city.
  3. Senso-ji Temple – Tokyo’s oldest Buddhist temple in Asakusa, surrounded by souvenir shops and street food.
  4. Meiji Shrine – A peaceful forested escape in the heart of the city.
  5. Tsukiji Outer Market – A paradise for fresh seafood lovers.
  6. Imperial Palace & East Gardens – A serene green space steeped in history.
  7. Akihabara – The center for anime, gaming, and electronics culture.
  8. Odaiba – A futuristic entertainment district on Tokyo Bay.
  9. Ueno Park & Zoo – Cherry blossoms in spring, museums year-round.
  10. Ginza – Tokyo’s upscale shopping and dining district.

Basic Japanese phrases for travelers

English Japanese
1 Hello / Good afternoon こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
2 Good morning おはようございます (Ohayou gozaimasu)
3 Good evening こんばんは (Konbanwa)
4 Thank you very much ありがとうございます (Arigatou gozaimasu)
5 Excuse me / Sorry すみません (Sumimasen)
6 Yes はい (Hai)
7 No いいえ (Iie)
8 I don’t understand わかりません (Wakarimasen)
9 Do you speak English? 英語は話せますか？ (Eigo wa hanasemasu ka?)
10 How are you? お元気ですか？ (Ogenki desu ka?)

Final Thoughts

Tokyo is a city you can visit again and again – every neighborhood has its own personality, and there’s always something new to discover. From ancient temples to futuristic skylines, Tokyo is a place where every corner holds a story.
