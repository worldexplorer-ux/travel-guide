Tips for First-Time Visitors

Dubai, one of the most popular travel destinations in the United Arab Emirates, is a city where tradition meets modern luxury. First-time visitors will be amazed by its record-breaking skyscrapers like the Burj Khalifa, massive shopping malls, and world-class dining. Beyond the modern skyline, Dubai also offers cultural experiences such as exploring traditional souks, visiting museums, and taking a desert safari.

    Things to Know:

  • Currency: UAE Dirham (AED)
  • Language: FArabic (English is widely spoken)
  • Plug Type: UK-style three-pin plug
  • Safety: Dubai is very safe for tourists
  • Tipping: Not required, but appreciated (5–10%)

Best Time to Visit Dubai

  • November to March - the weather is cooler and perfect for outdoor activities.
  • Summer (June to September) - is very hot, with temperatures often above 40°C (104°F), but many attractions and malls are air-conditioned.
  • Winter - is quieter and festive, with Christmas markets and lights.

    • Tip: Plan your trip between November and March for the most comfortable weather, and book early if you’re visiting during popular events like the Dubai Shopping Festival.

Getting Around Dubai

Dubai has a modern metro system that connects many major attractions. Taxis are also safe, reliable, and affordable. For a unique experience, try a water taxi on Dubai Creek. If you plan to explore outside the city, renting a car can be useful.

Must-See Attractions in Dubai

  1. Burj Khalifa – The tallest building in the world with breathtaking views.
  2. The Dubai Mall – A shopping paradise with an aquarium and ice rink.
  3. Dubai Fountain Show – A magical water and light show at the base of Burj Khalifa.
  4. Palm Jumeirah – An iconic man-made island with luxury resorts.
  5. Burj Al Arab – The famous sail-shaped luxury hotel.
  6. Dubai Marina – A stunning waterfront with restaurants, yachts, and nightlife.
  7. Desert Safari – A thrilling ride over golden dunes followed by a Bedouin-style dinner.
  8. Dubai Creek & Al Fahidi District – Explore old Dubai with its traditional souks and heritage sites.
  9. Jumeirah Mosque – One of the most beautiful mosques open to non-Muslim visitors.
  10. Atlantis Aquaventure Waterpark – Perfect for families and water adventure lovers.

The Dubai Mall

The Dubai Mall is one of the largest shopping centers in the world and a must-visit for tourists. It’s not just about shopping — inside, you’ll find an aquarium, an ice rink, cinemas, and hundreds of restaurants and cafés. Located next to the Burj Khalifa, it’s the perfect place to spend a full day indoors, especially during the hotter months.

Souk Madinat Jumeirah

Souk Madinat Jumeirah is a modern marketplace designed in the style of a traditional Arabian souk. Here, you’ll find shops selling souvenirs, jewelry, spices, and handmade crafts, along with many restaurants and bars. The souk also offers beautiful views of the Burj Al Arab, making it a great spot for both shopping and dining.

Global Village (Seasonal, Nov–Apr)

Global Village is a unique cultural and entertainment park that runs from November to April. It brings together pavilions from countries around the world, each offering food, shopping, and shows. It’s a family-friendly place where you can try different cuisines, watch live performances, and enjoy funfair rides — all in one evening.

Polite Arabic Words and Phrases for Travelers in Dubai

English Arabic
1 Hello السلام عليكم (As-salaam alaykum)
2 Reply to Hello وعليكم السلام (Wa alaykum as-salaam)
3 Welcome أهلاً وسهلاً (Ahlan wa sahlan)
4 Good morning صباح الخير (Sabah al-khayr)
5 Good evening مساء الخير (Masa’ al-khayr)
6 Thank you شكراً (Shukran)
7 Excuse me / Sorry عفواً / آسف (Afwan / Aasif)
8 Goodbye مع السلامة (Ma’a as-salama)

Traveler’s Tip: In Dubai, a warm smile and a simple Arabic greeting like “As-salaam alaykum” or “Shukran” can go a long way in showing respect and friendliness.
