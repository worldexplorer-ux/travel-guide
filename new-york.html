Tips for First-Time Visitors

New York City, often called “The Big Apple,” is one of the most famous cities in the world. First-time visitors will be amazed by its tall skyscrapers, lively streets, and endless attractions. From Times Square’s glowing lights to peaceful walks in Central Park, the city offers something for everyone. Beyond the landmarks, New York is also a cultural hub with museums, Broadway shows, and food from around the globe.

    Things to Know:

  • Currency: U.S. Dollar (USD)
  • Language: English (Spanish and many other languages are also common)
  • Plug Type: Type A and B (two flat pins, sometimes with a round grounding pin)
  • Safety: Generally safe in tourist areas, but always stay aware of your surroundings
  • Tipping: Expected in restaurants, bars, and taxis (15–20% is standard)

Best Time to Visit New York City

  • Spring (April–June) and Fall (September–November) are the best times to visit. The weather is pleasant, and there are many events and festivals.
  • Winter is magical with holiday decorations, but it can be very cold.
  • Summer is lively but hot and crowded.

Getting Around

The subway is the fastest way to travel around New York. Taxis and ride apps are easy to find, but can be expensive. Walking is also a great way to see the city, especially in Manhattan.

Must-See Attractions in New York City

  1. Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island – a symbol of freedom and history.
  2. Central Park – a huge green escape in the middle of the city.
  3. Times Square – bright lights and endless energy.
  4. Empire State Building – iconic views from the top.
  5. Broadway – world-class theater and musicals.
  6. 9/11 Memorial & Museum – a moving and important site.
  7. Brooklyn Bridge – walk across for amazing views of Manhattan.
  8. Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met) – one of the world’s greatest museums.
  9. Rockefeller Center & Top of the Rock – for views and seasonal events.
  10. Grand Central Terminal – stunning architecture and a busy hub.

Best Places to Eat in New York City

  1. Katz’s Delicatessen–A legendary Jewish-style deli on the Lower East Side known for its famous pastrami on rye.

  5. Delmonico’s– One of the oldest fine-dining institutions in the city (opened 1827), synonymous with classic American steakhouse elegance.

  6. The Corner Store– A trendy Soho favorite known for elevated comfort food.

  7. Junior's Restaurant & Bakery– Known for iconic New York–style cheesecake.

  8. Levain Bakery– Home of NYC’s very famous giant cookies.

  9. Russ & Daughters– A historic appetizing shop (est. 1914) famed for bagels, smoked salmon, and caviar.

Final Thoughts

New York is big, busy, and sometimes overwhelming, but that’s what makes it special. With famous landmarks, great food, and endless activities - it’s a city that truly has something for everyone.
